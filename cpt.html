Gain Experience While You Study

Curricular Practical Training (CPT) is work authorization for eligible F-1 students to pursue paid or unpaid off-campus internships that are part of their curriculum.

What is CPT?

CPT allows F-1 students to work off-campus in a role directly related to their major. It must be an integral part of your curriculum and approved by OISS before you begin.

  • Requires a job/internship offer
  • Must be employer- and date-specific
  • Applies to both paid and unpaid roles

📌 Key CPT Information

What is CPT?

Off-campus work authorization for F-1 students to gain practical experience in their major field of study. Must be integral to curriculum.

Authorization

Per semester; renewed each term.

Work Hours

Part-time ≤ 20 hrs/week (semester), Full-time > 20 hrs/week (breaks or with approval).

Part-Time CPT

Students may participate as much as needed for program requirements.

Full-Time CPT Limit

12 months of full-time CPT disqualifies OPT at same educational level.

Enrollment Requirement

Must be enrolled in at least one in-person or hybrid course. Summer: only internship course is allowed.

Eligibility

Valid F-1, 1+ academic year completed, CPT-tied course, advisor approval, full-time or RCL enrollment.

CPT in Final Semester

RCL + one in-person/hybrid course; CPT must count toward degree.

Application Steps

1. RCL request → 2. Log into Terra Dotta → 3. CPT Form + Offer → 4. Upload documents → 5. DSO approval & CPT I-20.

Changing Employers/Dates

Submit a new CPT request & get new I-20 before starting.

Other Notes

CPT is date/employer-specific. Paid & unpaid need approval. No USCIS/Unemployment limits.

📘 Key Points Summary

📘 What is CPT?

Work authorization for F-1 students to gain real-world experience related to their major. Must be integral to curriculum.

🕓 Work Hours

Part-time ≤ 20 hrs/week during semester; Full-time > 20 hrs/week during breaks or with approval.

📆 Authorization

Granted per semester. Must be renewed every term.

✅ Eligibility

  • Valid F-1 status
  • ≥ 1 academic year completed
  • Course tied to CPT
  • Advisor approval

🎓 Final Semester

Must have Reduced Course Load approval. Enroll in at least one hybrid or in-person course. CPT must count toward degree.

📋 Steps to Apply

RCL → Terra Dotta Login → Submit CPT Form + Offer → Upload Docs → DSO Approval

🔁 Change of Employer/Dates

Submit new CPT request. Get updated I-20 before working with new employer.

⚠️ Other Notes

CPT is employer- and date-specific. No USCIS approval needed. Required for both paid & unpaid internships.

📘 CPT Key Information

📘

What is CPT?

▶
Off-campus work authorization for F-1 students to gain practical experience in their major field of study. Must be integral to curriculum.
📅

Authorization

▶
CPT is authorized per semester and must be renewed each term.
⏰

Work Hours

▶
Part-time: ≤20 hrs/week during semesters. Full-time: >20 hrs/week during official breaks or with academic department approval.
🧑‍🎓

Part-Time CPT

▶
Students may participate in part-time CPT as much as needed to meet academic program requirements.
⛔

Full-Time CPT Limit

▶
12 months of full-time CPT will make a student ineligible for OPT at the same education level.
🧾

Enrollment Requirement

▶
Must be enrolled in at least one in-person or hybrid course. Fully online allowed ONLY if internship is the only remaining course.
📚

Eligibility

▶
Valid F-1 status, completed ≥1 academic year (unless immediate participation required), job offer related to major, course tied to CPT, advisor approval, and full-time enrollment (or RCL if final term).
🎓

CPT During Final Semester

▶
RCL approval is required. Must be enrolled in at least one in-person/hybrid course. CPT must count toward degree completion.
📝

Application Steps

▶
1. RCL request (if needed)
2. Log into Terra Dotta
3. Fill CPT Recommendation Form
4. Submit with job offer
5. Upload internship agreement & class schedule
6. DSO approves & issues CPT I-20
🔄

Changing Employers/Dates

▶
Notify OISS and submit a new CPT request in Terra Dotta. Must receive a new CPT I-20 before starting new job or new dates.
ℹ️

Other Notes

▶
CPT is employer- and date-specific. Both paid and unpaid internships require CPT authorization. USCIS approval is not required. No unemployment limit.

Terra Dotta CPT Process – Step-by-Step

Step 1: Login to Terra Dotta

Go to the Terra Dotta portal: https://eiu-isss.terradotta.com/_portal/.

Step 2

Select New and Current EIU Studnet Login and login using your EIU Credentials.

Step 3: Access Control Center

Once logged in, click on the Control Center from the dashboard. This is where all student actions and requests are managed.

Step 4: Click on the CPT Request widget.

Select on Course Reduce Form
